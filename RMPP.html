Research Methods and Proffesional Practice

This module focuses on equipping you with practical research methods for real-world settings. It covers a range of tools and techniques essential for researchers, and includes continuous feedback through case studies and project tasks. Additionally, the module delves into important aspects like legal, social, ethical, and professional issues related to computing projects, alongside relevant codes of practice.

Learning Outcomes

On completion of this module I will be able to:

Appraise the professional, legal, social, cultural and ethical issues that affect computing professionals.
Appraise the principles of academic investigation, applying them to a research topic in the applicable computing field.
Evaluate critically existing literature, research design and methodology for the chosen topic, including data analysis processes.
Produce and evaluate critically a research proposal for the chosen topic.

Data Collection Methods

Case Studies
Case studies involve in-depth examination of individuals or groups, leading to hypotheses and a broader understanding of phenomena while cautioning against determining causation due to potential researcher bias and atypical respondents.

Focus Groups
Focus groups provide a qualitative approach where 6-10 individuals with common backgrounds express insights to answer fundamental "why," "what," and "how" questions.

Quantitative Observation
Quantitative observation focuses on numerical data collected objectively and analysed statistically, often through experiments or surveys.

Qualitative Observation
Qualitative observation is centered on monitoring characteristics and behaviors of predetermined respondents in natural settings, offering in-depth insights.
